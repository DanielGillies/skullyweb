ABOUT US

Skully pioneers advanced situational awareness technology. The company was founded in 2013 with the goal of revolutionizing the most frequent, ubiquitous, and dangerous aspect of our lives: transportation.

Skully’s human-centered approach to design and technology has led to the development of the Skully AR-1, the first intelligent HUD motorcycle helmet for consumers.

Skully is a privately held company with strategic partnerships spanning the world’s leading automotive technology companies.

Team
Marcus Weller
CEO

A visionary savant, Marcus Weller, Co-Founder & Chairman of Skully Inc., is a motorcycle fanatic with an obsession for human-centered technology and design. Marcus spent many years working in the automotive and semiconductor industries and was quite content to keep doing so until a motorcycle accident inspired him to form Skully in 2013. His innovations have been lauded by the likes of CNN, Popular Science, USA Today, and Popular Mechanics.

Mitchell Weller
COO

Mitchell Weller has played an integral role in turning the idea of the SKULLY AR-1 into a reality. As Co-Founder and Chief of Staff, Mitchell architects, implements, and manages business operations automation solutions for SKULLY. Prior to joining SKULLY, Mitchell served in the U.S. Army as a Human Resources and Operations Specialist, completing two tours in Iraq. Mitchell has also held Director positions in human resources, operations, and material logistics. Mitchell holds a BA in Psychology from the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities and is a motorcycle enthusiast, sharing his brother’s passion for enhancing motorcycle safety through technology.

Josh Bloom
VP Product

Josh Bloom is currently the Vice President of Product Design at Skully. He spent his career working for the likes of Samsung and Frogdesign focused on mobile phones, tablets, wearables, and room scale computing. Josh joined Skully to make the motorsports industry safer for everyone, including designing heads up display motorcycle helmets that look awesome while keeping riders safe.
